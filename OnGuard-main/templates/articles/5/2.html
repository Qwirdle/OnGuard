Encryption is a way to protect information via scrambling it into a certain code by using a certain algorithm, typically containing a public key and decrypting it via a private key.

Encryption has been used by even war generals in Roman times. A form of symmetric encryption (meaning private and public keys are the same) back then was a “Caesar Cipher”. To encrypt something using it, one would take a string of letters and words and shift every letter by a certain amount. If the key is 1 right, to decrypt it you would shift every letter 1 left

Unfortunately for hackers, black-hat or otherwise, modern ciphers are not as easy to brute force as this. Many are asymmetrical now, meaning they have a separate public and private key, leading to more applications like digital signatures which allow people to authenticate themselves by encrypting a message with a private key and giving others a public key which can only decrypt things from their private key.

An example of asymmetrical encryption is a Hash Function, which takes an input of a variable length and turns it into a fixed length string. SHA-256 is an example of this, turning a given input into a 256-bit long string

People with a private key will become bigger targets for social engineering. It is important to make sure they are trained to deal with such problems. Background checks are also important to not give them to untrustworthy bad actors
