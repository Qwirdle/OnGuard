Workers, depending on what they work as, may be asked to change settings in certain apps or sites.

These include:

  • Clearing cookies and caches to make certain data not get logged

    • Cookies and Caches are ways data is stored in a browser, they can be used for things like storing images to make a site load faster or saving a shopping cart. They can, some times, include things like session tokens (used by sites to log in quickly), which can be a cybersecurity issue

  • Changing settings in social media

    • This includes mainly settings that disable data being shared to third parties.

  • Data minimization

    • Making sure only the minimum data is provided when signing up to sites or apps

  • Encrypting data

    • Designs for keycards

    • IDs

    • Legal documents

  • Strong passwords

    • Some organizations even require employees to change passwords often to minimize any current passwords to be found in data breaches.

  • Public networks/devices

    • Do not use public devices or networks (at an internet cafe, for example) to log into accounts or to share confidential information.

Many more things may be included. Restrictions may be higher if an organization is especially secretive (government agencies, for example) or be virtually non-existent if it doesn't matter (a cashier, per se).

Summary

  • Make sure settings in sites and apps are set to not share data

  • Have strong passwords

  • Never use public devices or networks anywhere to log in or send data
