3.1 Recognizing Suspicious Emails and Links

Being able to recognize and safely deal with suspicious emails and links could prevent severe cyberattacks. These are very common methods of attacks, and sometimes they can be very hard to identify. However, because of the danger associated with malicious emails and links it's extremely important to understand the signs that a link or email is dangerous.

Common Identifiers of Suspicious Emails:

  • Unfamiliar Sender: The email is from someone who you don't know or a company that you aren't aware of. The email address itself could also be the same as one of your coworkers or a company but with a slight change in the characters.

  • Urgency:The email creates a sense of stress or importance in order to get you to act less logically when interacting with it. An example of this is an email saying that your account will be deleted if you don't log in through this link.

  • Poor Spelling or Grammar: If an email has improper grammar or structure it means that it is more likely to be from a malicious sender who doesn't understand the language that they are sending the email in. Branding/logo looking off in design or position can also be a similar sign.

  • Asks for Sensitive Info: Companies will never ask for your sensitive information over email, like logins and such, so if anyone asks for your sensitive information it's very likely to be for malicious purposes.

If you've encountered a suspicious email, let your IT department know. Do not interact with anything on the email, as even opening a link to a website without entering anything into it can cause malware to spread into your company's network.

Summary

  • Suspicious emails may have unfamiliar senders, urgency, or request sensitive info.

  • Check links by hovering, avoid shortened URLs, and be cautious with unusual attachments.

  • Report to IT and do not interact.
